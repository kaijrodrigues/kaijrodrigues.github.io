Brand Tracker: Sportsbooks, Event Contracts & Prediction Markets

Brand Tracking Google Trends R Shiny 2026

Overview

Prediction markets stopped being a niche curiosity in 2024. Polymarket cleared roughly $9 billion in trading volume on the year, most of it riding the US presidential election. Kalshi won a landmark court ruling in September 2024 that cleared it to list political event contracts, sending its volumes climbing into 2025. Sportsbooks still owned the category by share, but the ground was moving fast.

To see how that shift was landing with consumers, I built a five-stage brand funnel tracker across all three competing categories: legal sportsbooks, CFTC-regulated event contracts, and prediction markets. It runs on a synthetic survey dataset I generated with Claude Sonnet 4.6 (monthly reads, July 2024 through June 2025), paired with real Google Trends data, and tests whether search interest leads or lags brand health.

Note: the survey figures are a methods demonstration, generated to show the reasoning a live tracker enables, not to report real market share. The Google Trends data is real.

Approach

The dashboard is built in R with Shiny and deployed via Shinylive, so it runs entirely in the browser with no server.

  • Screening: Every respondent is a past-month sports bettor or event-contract trader, so each read reflects active participants, not the general public
  • Brand funnel: Five-stage funnel (Aided Awareness → Consideration → Registration → P1M Betting → Preferred Brand) tracked monthly across seven platforms
  • Search interest: Google Trends relative search volume overlaid against awareness to surface lead/lag dynamics
  • Platforms: DraftKings, FanDuel, bet365, PrizePicks, Kalshi, Robinhood, PolyMarket
  • Filters: Age band, gender, region, income, and bettor type (casual vs. heavy, based on self-reported frequency of wagering activity), all applied dynamically

Dashboard

Two tabs, Brand Funnel and Search Interest, both responding live to the sidebar filters.

Business Implications

A year of monthly reads surfaces patterns a single snapshot would miss:

  • Kalshi's gap isn't awareness, it's trust. Aided awareness doubles over the year (33% to 60%), but only about 57% of aware users move on to consideration, against roughly 80% for DraftKings. The leak sits at the top of the funnel, where credibility and education matter more than reach.
  • The election permanently lifted the prediction-market floor. P1M betting roughly triples into the November 2024 election (Kalshi 6% to 16%, Polymarket 8% to 18%), then holds at about 63% of peak by February 2025. The spike fades, but active usage settles well above where it began.
  • Young bettors are the prediction-market base, and they're underserved. Under-35s bet with Kalshi and Polymarket at 2.5 to 3 times the rate of the 45-and-up group, while DraftKings and FanDuel stay flat across age. The incumbents have no age advantage to defend in event contracts, leaving a young, engaged audience wide open to targeted digital media and ambassador-led marketing.
  • Prediction markets win trial but not loyalty. Among people who actually bet with them, only about 40% name a prediction market their preferred brand, versus roughly 55% for DraftKings and FanDuel. The shortfall is about preference and retention, not just scale.
  • The largest single opening is the undecided. About 17% of casual bettors name no preferred brand at all, a bigger bloc than any one challenger's share. That preference is still unclaimed.
  • The incumbents are nearly out of headroom. DraftKings and FanDuel sit around 94% awareness with only 4 to 5 points of yearly growth left. Their next gains have to come from conversion and defending preference, not new reach.
