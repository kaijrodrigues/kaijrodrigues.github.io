Brand Tracker: Sportsbooks, Event Contracts & Prediction Markets

Brand Tracking Google Trends R Shiny 2026

Overview

Between July 2024 and June 2025, the prediction market space hit a real inflection point. Kalshi and Polymarket both saw substantial revenue growth over the period: Kalshi following a landmark court ruling that cleared it to offer political event contracts, Polymarket posting record volume around the 2024 US election. Traditional sportsbooks kept their lead by share, but the competitive landscape was shifting fast.

This project tracks that shift across legal sportsbooks, event contracts, and prediction markets. The dataset is an AI-generated synthetic brand health survey built using Claude Sonnet 4.6, producing monthly reads for each platform from July 2024 through June 2025, paired with real Google Trends data sourced over the same period. Together they power a five-stage consumer funnel tracker asking: who is winning mindshare, and where are the gaps?

Approach

The tracker runs on two data sources: a synthetic brand health survey generated using Claude Sonnet 4.6 (monthly waves, July 2024 through June 2025) and real Google Trends data pulled for the same window. The interactive dashboard was built in R with Shiny and deployed via Shinylive, so it runs entirely in the browser.

  • Brand funnel: Five-stage funnel (Aided Awareness → Consideration → Registration → P1M Betting → Preferred Brand) tracked monthly across seven platforms
  • Search interest: Google Trends relative search volume overlaid against awareness to surface lead/lag dynamics
  • Platforms: DraftKings, FanDuel, bet365, PrizePicks, Kalshi, Robinhood, PolyMarket
  • Filters: Age band, gender, region, income, and bettor type (casual vs. heavy, based on self-reported frequency of wagering activity), all applied dynamically

Dashboard

The dashboard has two tabs: Brand Funnel and Search Interest. Use the sidebar to select a brand, apply demographic filters, and explore how funnel performance shifts across segments.

Business Implications

A brand tracker like this surfaces signals a one-time study can't:

  • Funnel leaks tell you where to invest. A gap between Consideration and Registration is a product or trust problem, not an awareness problem. That distinction changes where budget goes.
  • Know where growth is coming from. Are prediction market gains pulling in new bettors, or pulling away from sportsbooks? The answer is decisive for how you position and where you compete.
  • Search spikes are an early warning. Where Trends leads awareness, brands can get ahead of rising salience with timely media and creative before it shows up in survey data.
  • Young prediction market users are an underserved advertising opportunity. Kalshi and Polymarket show notably higher Registration and P1M Betting rates among younger cohorts than traditional sportsbooks. This audience is engaged and growing, making it well-suited for targeted digital campaigns and brand ambassador programs.
