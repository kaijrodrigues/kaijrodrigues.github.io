Brand Tracker: Sportsbooks, Event Contracts & Prediction Markets

Brand Tracking Google Trends R Shiny 2026

Overview

The US sports betting and prediction markets landscape has fragmented rapidly — legal sportsbooks, CFTC-regulated event contracts, and decentralized prediction markets now compete for overlapping audiences, yet they are rarely analyzed side by side. This project asks a straightforward brand health question: who is winning consumer mindshare, and why?

The goal was to build a repeatable tracking system that surfaces funnel movement — from awareness through to preferred brand — at the individual platform level, and layers in Google Trends data to understand whether search interest is a leading or lagging indicator of brand health shifts.

Approach

The tracker was built in two parts: a synthetic brand health survey panel and a Google Trends pull, both structured as longitudinal wave data. The interactive dashboard was built entirely in R using Shiny and deployed as a static app via Shinylive, requiring no server infrastructure.

  • Brand funnel: Five-stage consumer funnel (Aided Awareness → Consideration → Registration → P1M Betting → Preferred Brand) tracked across seven platforms and multiple demographic cuts
  • Search interest: Google Trends relative search volume indexed by platform, overlaid against awareness to evaluate lead/lag dynamics
  • Platforms tracked: DraftKings, FanDuel, bet365, PrizePicks, Kalshi, Robinhood, PolyMarket
  • Filters: Age band, gender, region, income, and bettor type — all applied dynamically to every chart in the dashboard

Business Implications

Brand trackers of this structure surface several commercially actionable signals that a one-time study cannot:

  • Funnel leakage by stage: A large gap between Consideration and Registration typically points to a friction or trust barrier at the product or regulatory level — not a brand awareness problem. Correctly diagnosing this changes where investment goes.
  • Category momentum: Tracking all three categories together reveals whether growth in prediction markets is coming from net-new bettors or from cannibalization of sportsbook share — a critical distinction for competitive strategy.
  • Search as an early signal: Where search interest consistently leads brand awareness, platforms can use Trends spikes as an early warning system — either to capitalize on rising salience or to manage reputational events before they register in survey data.
  • Demographic targeting: Funnel conversion rates vary substantially by age band and bettor type, informing where acquisition spend is most efficient and where retention efforts should focus.
